research overview
it sets the theoritcal foundation for the proposal

The introduction of interactive architecture in the schoolyard environment is the main approach of the research. Initially, the concept of interactive architecture is approached, the factors that make up the concept, namely the user, the designer and the materiality, are deconstructed, future possibilities are listed and finally implemented examples are given.

Then the concept of the schoolyard, primary school, and its manifestations as a means of education and as a means of play is approached and examples are given. At the same time, the research conducted examines how first-grade children form autonomous spatial units and how they structure the relationships between these and their environment, which in this case is the schoolyard.

The research will have two stages. The individual one which is small scale, takes the form of a short craft and takes place in the classroom. The group one, which touches the physical scale of the children, takes place in the schoolyard and is in the form of play and painting.

Also through the research, issues of inclusive design are examined. Finally, conclusions about the interactive schoolyard are listed.

diploma overview


This project explores the possibility of creating an interactive architectural installation for children in schoolyards, based on the theoretical background of the research project "Interactive architecture in schoolyards". The installation consists of a modular repetitive (modular) static structure applying typologies developed during the research in the school and a dynamic origami surface that changes in response to stimuli from its environment.

At the same time the data collected from the children's activity is processed using machine learning and creative code techniques and displayed in the installation through projections influencing the user and the installation itself. The project will cover the process of designing and building the installation at scale using 3D printing and microcontroller technologies and evaluating its performance and impact. The aim of the installation is to provide a playful and educational environment for children, and to demonstrate the potential of interactive architecture to improve the quality of the school environment.
